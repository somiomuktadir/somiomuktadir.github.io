Our Revolution: A short stage play

"One day, we will rise, defiant. From the grave, we rise. Our wronged spirits will forever haunt you and tear down your arrogance."

Scene 01

The revolution leader, Eram, sat across the table from the opposition. The air in the opulent meeting room was thick with pretense.

— So, Mr. Eram. A drink?

— I do not drink, thank you.

— Let us proceed then. Your friends have already agreed to our terms.

— What makes you think I'd fall for your pathetic charade?

— You are a brave man, Eram, but bravery quickly bleeds into foolishness. Bring Ms. Tasnim in.

Eram’s world dissolved. Tasnim, his confidante, the keeper of his dreams, and the other half of a heart-shaped locket he had gifted her, walked in. The President, observing Eram's silent pain, pressed the advantage.

— Why the look of shock, Mr. Eram? Ms. Tasnim is clever; she knows how to seize a moment. The tides have turned against you, but we are prepared to help improve the scenario—though not without your cooperation.

— Do you value time more than life?

— I value my country and prefer peace.

— DO YOU?!

— Yes. What do you prefer, Mr. Eram?

— I prefer the truth.

With a deep breath, Eram was offered the agreement. His answer was a definitive act of defiance: he ripped the agreement in half. The President, unmoved, merely offered a cold dismissal: "Very well, you are free to leave, Mr. Eram." Eram walked away, already plotting how to regroup.

Moments later, the President gave his final orders to the Chief of Military Intelligence: "You know what you have to do."

Scene 02

On the streets, Eram rallied his loyal followers, desperation fueling their courage. The revolution had reached its final battleground.

— This is the last line, brothers! We fight to the last breath. We won't die like cowards—we will enter history as the brave few who stood against tyranny while the rest cowered. ARE WE COWARDS?

— NO, THEY ARE!

— Move, everyone! Move now! We are with you, Eram! The brave people stand with us, folks. Move fast! We are blocking the Jatrabari road.

There are moments when fate withdraws its smile from the brave. These moments often descend into the shadows of history—a history that conceals the truth within multiple, deceptive layers. A mass of armed police, led by IG John, waited for the protesters. History had never before recorded such a violent crash.

Scene 03

The night was a tapestry of grief, stitched with the sounds of pain and horror. The Jatrabari crossroads were painted crimson—the hue of passion, love, and courage. Countless lifeless bodies lay scattered, but the President was unmoved.

He recorded his address before leaving his office:

— "Esteemed citizens. It is with the deepest grief that I announce our nation has been violently thrust into instability once again. I mournfully regret the bloodshed, and the opposition's evil perpetrators will be brought to swift justice. A week-long curfew takes effect tomorrow morning. Have patience. Long live our country, long live our great leaders!"

Scene 04

Eram drags himself up from the pile of the dead, feeling nothing but a crushing need for survival. He walks across the vacant roads and knocks on a door, desperate for help. A young girl, Ema, opens the door, her expression utterly bewildered. Eram stares, unable to look away—her face is a ghost of Tasnim's.

— Miss, please, could you help me? (Eram, crying out in pain.)

— (She gives him a confused glance, as if she cannot see him at all.)

— Ema, shut that door! SHUT THE DOOR! Who is out there? (A voice screams from inside.)

— No one, Ma.

The door slams shut. He turns away, utterly dejected, and walks back to the place from which he came, only to see his own lifeless, bloody body being carted away from the pile of the dead. Days later, another body was found, with a half-heart locket around her neck, clutching a small note that read: "I didn't betray, I chose you."
